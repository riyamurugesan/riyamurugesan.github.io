Research Statement

Transcatheter aortic valve replacement (TAVR) is a well-established treatment for patients with aortic stenosis, but carries the risk of paravalvular leak (PVL) around the prosthetic valve. My previous research has illustrated that with newer generation valves, less aggressive pre-procedural sizing is possible without an increase in PVL rates. Building on this work, my future research aims to computationally model the heart to account for patient-specific anatomy and improve valve sizing strategies. I hope to refine pre-procedural planning and minimize the risk of paravalvular leak and thrombosis for patients.
