Research Statement

Transcatheter aortic valve replacement (TAVR) is a well-established treatment for patients with aortic stenosis, but carries the risk of paravalvular leak (PVL) and disturbed blood flow around the prosthetic valve. My previous research has illustrated that with newer generation valves, less aggressive pre-procedural sizing is possible without an increase in PVL rates. Building on this work, my future research aims to computationally model the heart to account for patient-specific anatomy and improve valve sizing strategies. I hope to refine pre-procedural planning and minimize the risk of paravalvular leak and atherosclerosis for patients.


Bautch Lab

The Bautch lab’s major focus is on the endothelial cells that line blood vessels and their behavioral responses to disease and shear stress.

Cardiovascular Modeling and Simulation Lab

The Cardiovascular Modeling and Simulation lab’s major focus is to use computational and mathematical methods to develop physiological models of cardiovascular function in health and disease.
